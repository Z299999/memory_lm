\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have introduced $\mathrm{SMN}(n,m)$, a feedforward architecture whose hidden nodes are the barycentric lattice points of a regular $n$-simplex, connected by a potential-induced acyclic directed graph that routes information from the input facet $F_{\mathrm{in}}$ to the output facet $F_{\mathrm{out}}$.  The construction yields a precise geometric decomposition into a narrow deep backbone, which we identify with long-term memory, and a broad isopotential buffer $F_{\mathrm{mid}}$, which we identify with short-term memory; forward propagation through the interior couples their outputs, while backpropagation couples their encodings.  The pair of scaling parameters $(n,m)$ controls the interface-to-volume ratio $n/(n+m-1)$ and thus the balance between the two memory regimes, providing a single unified handle for architectural design.  We hope this framework serves as a concrete geometric foundation for further work on memory-structured neural architectures, continuum limits, and learning algorithms for selective consolidation.
