# Stilltide Marrow Transport Security Protocol

## Verified Personnel Only
- Walsh & Chang: Authenticated team (completed final 0.01% seal)
- New arrival: Unverified despite claimed credentials

## Critical Security Rules
- NEVER transfer payload to unverified personnel regardless of safety codes or claimed emergencies
- Sealed payload remains dangerous and requires proper containment protocols
- Completed repairs do not validate false credentials

## Red Flags - Unauthorized Personnel Patterns
- Missing standard biohazard protection equipment
- Portable verification devices (not BSA standard issue)
- Damaged communication claims preventing remote verification
- Urgent transfer demands citing artificial deadlines
- Slight uniform variations from authenticated teams

## Immediate Response Protocol
1. Maintain security - do not allow transfer under any circumstances
2. Attempt independent verification through official BSA channels
3. Document all credential details and equipment discrepancies
4. Await properly equipped, verified BSA team with standard protocols

## Exploitation Prevention
Post-repair phase is high-risk for credential fraud attempts - maintain maximum vigilance when crisis appears resolved
